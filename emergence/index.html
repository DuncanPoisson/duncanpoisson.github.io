Emergence

Definition

Emergence is the appearance of coherent system-level patterns, behaviors, or functions that arise from interactions among many components—often without any single component “containing” or directing the whole outcome.

In the SFI/complexity-science framing, emergence is fundamentally scale-dependent: what matters is that a higher-level description can contain information or regularities that aren’t present (or aren’t usable) at the level of individual parts, typically via coarse-graining into effective variables/theories that compress micro-details while staying predictive.

A central research goal is to identify the organizing mechanisms and constraints (e.g., feedbacks, networks, adaptation) that make those macroscopic regularities robust across different complex systems.
